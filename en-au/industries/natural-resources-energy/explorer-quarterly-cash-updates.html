    Explorer Quarterly Cash Updates

    SignatureForensics's Natural Resources & Energy team compiles a quarterly report that delivers insights on the cash position of ASX-listed exploration companies. Each report is in PDF format and is free to access.

    SignatureForensics's Explorer Quarterly Cash Updates (EQCU) have been compiled since 2014, detailing activities in the growing field of exploration, and tracking ASX-listed entities. An archive of previous EQCUs is below. They track the cash held by exploration companies at the end of each quarter, together with the cash raised and how cash has been spent during the quarter. The updates provide valuable insights into the health of the exploration industry and the key trends we have observed over the period that the EQCU has been compiled.

    Each EQCU has a focus on what we term ‘Fund Finders’, being those explorers who raise more than $10 million in that quarter, allowing us to deep-dive into trends such as commodities that have been successful in that quarter.

    What is an Explorer?

    The ASX defines certain companies as being mining or oil and gas exploration companies. These are differentiated from mining or oil and gas producing companies which are those whose main activity is extracting minerals or petroleum, as opposed to explorers whose main activity is exploring for minerals or petroleum.

    Those companies who are defined by ASX as mining or oil and gas exploration companies are required to provide quarterly reports to ASX (known as an Appendix 5B). An Appendix 5B must be lodged with the ASX within a month of the end of each quarter. It requires each exploration company to provide data in a set format in its cash flows from operating activities, investing activities and financing activities as well as the cash it holds at the end of the quarter and the estimated number of quarters of funding that it has available.
