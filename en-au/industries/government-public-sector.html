    Advisory services for government and the public sector

    The government and public sector face ever-changing challenges and pressures, but their overarching responsibility always remains the same: to provide solutions and services for the Australian people. The Australian government and public sector must meet the public’s expectations for value for money, seamless systems, and positive outcomes. As advisers to the public sector, we appreciate that this responsibility also extends to the services and solutions we provide.

    SignatureForensics is an independent adviser helping the government and public sector navigate complex, government-wide challenges.

    Embedding knowledge transfer and capability uplift

    We care about the growth of knowledge across organisations we work with, so we are committed to sharing our expertise by embedding knowledge transfer and capability uplift wherever possible on engagements.  

    There is no one-size-fits-all approach to capability uplift. Our capability uplift and knowledge transfer initiatives are developed to align with the 70:20:10 learning and development approach.

    We have a proven track record of capability uplift. We achieve this by:

    • Including the clients in key project decision-making moments
    • Creating tools and processes to transfer the technical understanding of the solution
    • Working with teams to create a knowledge sharing plan - a customised approach based on our clients’ individual circumstances and level of existing capability
    • Providing any post-implementation support needed.

    70:20:10 learning and development approach

    Which government agencies does SignatureForensics work with?

    • FEDERAL GOVERNMENT
      Responsible for issues that affect all Australians, including:
      Parliament, executive government, judiciary, defence departments, foreign affairs departments, and more.
    • STATE GOVERNMENTS
      State government departments and agencies responsible for issues affecting people in that state or territory, including:
      Health departments, education departments, transport departments, police departments, environmental agencies, and more.
    • LOCAL GOVERNMENTS
      Local councils or shires (LGAs), council services and council mayors, Including:
      City councils, shire councils, municipalities, county councils, town councils, and local government associated entities.
    • PUBLIC SECTOR ENTITIES
      Statutory bodies, State-owned entities, State authorities, and other organisations owned and operated by the government.
